NVIDIA GPU Series Overview

RTX 50 Series

The latest generation released in early 2025, the RTX 50 series brings advanced 4th-gen ray tracing cores and 5th-gen Tensor cores for AI and DLSS 4. Key models like the RTX 5090 offer up to 32GB GDDR7 VRAM for extreme 4K gaming and demanding creative workloads. This generation focuses on efficiency and performance improvements, supporting new technologies like AV1 encoding and PCIe 5.0.

RTX 40 Series

Launched in 2022-2023, the RTX 40 series introduced 3rd-gen RT cores and DLSS 3, enhancing ray tracing quality and frame rates. Flagship cards like the RTX 4090 cater to 4K and 8K gamers and content creators, while models like the RTX 4060/4070 focus on 1080p and 1440p gaming with strong efficiency. Super variants provide incremental performance upgrades.

RTX 30 Series

Released between 2020-2022, Ampere brought 2nd-gen ray tracing and Tensor cores with DLSS 2. The RTX 3090 Ti was a powerful choice for 4K enthusiasts and professionals. RTX 3060 and 3060 Ti offered great performance for budget-conscious gamers targeting 1080p and 1440p.

RTX 20 Series

This first generation of RTX GPUs introduced real-time ray tracing and AI-based DLSS but featured 1st-gen cores with limited efficiency compared to successors. The RTX 2080 Ti led the pack for high-end gaming, with lower models like RTX 2060 offering entry-level ray tracing.

GTX 16 Series

The GTX 16 series targeted gamers who wanted good 1080p performance without the added cost of RTX features, lacking ray tracing and Tensor cores but offering efficient traditional rasterization performance.

NVIDIA GPU Model Number Breakdown

"GeForce" is NVIDIA's gaming graphics line. The prefixes denote technology features:

  • RTX: Supports ray tracing and AI features like DLSS.
  • GTX: Traditional raster graphics, no ray tracing hardware.
  • GT: Entry-level or basic graphics.

The Generation Number is the first two digits after the prefix and specifies the architecture and technological generation. For example:

  • RTX 30xx = 30 series (Ampere architecture)
  • RTX 40xx = 40 series (Ada Lovelace)
  • RTX 50xx = 50 series (Blackwell, the latest)

The Model Tier Number (the next two digits) indicates the performance tier within that generation:

  • 90 = Enthusiast/flagship tier
  • 80 = High-end
  • 70 = Upper mid-range
  • 60 = Mid-range
  • 50 and below = Entry-level or budget

Suffixes add more specifics about variants:

  • Ti: Higher-performance variant of a base model, usually positioned between the base and the next tier (e.g., RTX 4070 Ti).
  • SUPER: Mid-cycle refresh with upgraded specs such as faster clocks or additional cores.
  • M: Mobile GPU version for laptops.

Example breakdown for RTX 4070 Ti: "RTX" means ray tracing capable GPU, "40" means 40th generation Ada Lovelace architecture, "70" indicates upper mid-range tier, and "Ti" shows it is a boosted performance variant over the regular RTX 4070.
