Intel Core Series Overview

Core i3

  • Entry-level CPUs designed for budget and everyday computing.
  • Typically 4 cores and 8 threads with no efficiency cores in recent generations.
  • Suitable for web browsing, office work, media playback, and casual gaming.
  • Includes integrated UHD graphics for displays without the need for a discrete GPU.

Core i5

  • Mid-range CPUs balancing solid performance with efficiency.
  • Usually 6 to 14 cores with a mix of performance and efficiency cores, supporting 12 to 20 threads.
  • Popular for mainstream gaming, multitasking, and moderate content creation.
  • Often paired with discrete GPUs but have capable integrated UHD graphics.

Core i7

  • High-performance processors aimed at enthusiasts and professional users.
  • Typically 8 to 20 cores (performance + efficiency) with 16 to 28 threads.
  • Excels in high-end gaming, streaming, video editing, and other demanding workloads.
  • Integrated UHD graphics available, though most high-end setups use discrete GPUs.

Core i9

  • Premium enthusiast and professional-grade CPUs with up to 24 cores and 32 threads.
  • Designed for extreme multitasking, professional workstations, 4K/VR gaming, and heavy rendering.
  • Highest clock speeds and largest cache sizes with higher power consumption.
  • Usually require discrete GPUs, though some workstation models offer integrated graphics.
